Conceptual art was revolving mostly around the idea of art – the idea which still remains hopeless undefined. Since at least 1986* we know that a machine is able to establish network of concepts in surprisingly human way. In spite of recent advances in AI, sentient machines will be still a science fiction prop for the foreseeable future. However, is art something exclusively human or could be simulated? In other words - is it computable or not?

Despite its age, Heidegger's writing about art remains an important account of phenomenon which escapes from a scientific lawfulness. We can safely assume that machines will keep gather knowledge about us by means of acquisition and processing of data. Then we can ask what will be the origin of art form their standpoint. The formal axis of the work is a language model constructed on Martin Heidegger's "Der Ursprung des Kunstwerkes".

The screen no.1 shows a gravity drift in a semantic space - words are grouped according to their meanings. The screen no.2 consists of a real-time output from a generative model. The accompanying sound comes from the synchronization of emerging consecutively words of the text with a sonification of their representations in vector space.
