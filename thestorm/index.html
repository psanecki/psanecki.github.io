The Storm That Shelters the Carnival from Injury in the Tent is a software installation which brings a viewer closer to a volatility and an unpredictability of global financial forces. Visual data-structures and spectral sound synthesis are anchored in a life market data. The title of the work plays with political buzzwords that are intended to convince masses about an urgent necessity of defeating exterior threads.

Apart from offering phenomenological insights into a nature of the economic abstraction, the installation poses questions concerning cultural and socioeconomic potentiality that emerges at the meeting of fine arts and open-source movement.

Nowadays, global economic turbulences are indiscernible from environmental and political ones. We must adapt to an everlasting instability and find our place within it. Behind its aesthetic facets, work of art is an emancipation device, at least if an artist follows the revolutionary spirit of the 20th century avant-garde.

Pure-data, which is lingua franca of computer music and sound art, is used in a way that blurs a distinction between trading instruments and those belonging to a radical music. The installation consists implementations of many financial indicators well known from exchange markets. Therefore, one can imagine The Storm That Shelters... as a genuine assistance during undertaking financial speculations.
